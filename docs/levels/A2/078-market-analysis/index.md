# Marktanalyse / Market Analysis

---

## 001: Marktanalyse durchführen / Conducting Market Analysis

**Situation:**  
Anna führt eine Marktanalyse durch.  
*Anna conducts a market analysis.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Anna:**  
Ich führe eine Marktanalyse durch.  
*I'm conducting a market analysis.*

**Kollege:**  
Was analysierst du?  
*What are you analyzing?*

**Anna:**  
Den Markt für unsere Produkte.  
*The market for our products.*

**Kollege:**  
Das ist wichtig.  
*That's important.*

**Anna:**  
Ja, ich sammle Daten über Kunden und Konkurrenz.  
*Yes, I'm collecting data about customers and competition.*

**Kollege:**  
Das ist eine gute Idee.  
*That's a good idea.*

**Anna:**  
So kann ich Trends erkennen.  
*That way I can recognize trends.*

**Kollege:**  
Das hilft bei der Planung.  
*That helps with planning.*

**Anna:**  
Ja, genau.  
*Yes, exactly.*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Modal Verbs (müssen, können, sollen)

**English:** Modal verbs express necessity, ability, or obligation. They are used with another verb in the infinitive form.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Wir müssen vorsichtig sein** - We need to be careful
- **Wir können die Risiken managen** - We can manage the risks
- **Wir sollten den Markt segmentieren** - We should segment the market
- **Wir müssen unsere Strategie anpassen** - We need to adapt our strategy

### Perfekt (Past Tense)

**English:** The Perfekt tense describes completed actions in the past, formed with "haben" or "sein" + past participle.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich habe Marktchancen identifiziert** - I've identified market opportunities
- **Der Markt hat sich verändert** - The market has changed
- **Ich habe analysiert** - I've analyzed

### Accusative Case with Direct Objects

**English:** The accusative case is used for direct objects in German sentences.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich führe eine Marktanalyse durch** - I conduct a market analysis (accusative)
- **Ich sammle Daten** - I collect data (accusative)
- **Ich analysiere die Kundenbedürfnisse** - I analyze customer needs (accusative)

### Subordinate Clauses with "dass" and "weil"

**English:** Subordinate clauses provide additional information and are introduced by conjunctions like "dass" (that) or "weil" (because).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich denke, dass wir handeln müssen** - I think that we need to act
- **Es ist wichtig, weil der Markt sich verändert** - It's important because the market is changing
