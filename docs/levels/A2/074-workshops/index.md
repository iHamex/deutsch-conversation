# Workshops / Workshops

---

## 001: Workshop auswählen / Selecting Workshop

**Situation:**  
Anna wählt einen Workshop aus.  
*Anna selects a workshop.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Anna:**  
Ich suche nach einem Workshop.  
*I'm looking for a workshop.*

**Kollege:**  
Welches Thema interessiert dich?  
*Which topic interests you?*

**Anna:**  
Ich interessiere mich für Zeitmanagement.  
*I'm interested in time management.*

**Kollege:**  
Das ist ein gutes Thema.  
*That's a good topic.*

**Anna:**  
Gibt es Workshops in unserer Firma?  
*Are there workshops in our company?*

**Kollege:**  
Ja, wir haben verschiedene Angebote.  
*Yes, we have various offers.*

**Anna:**  
Das ist gut. Wo kann ich mich informieren?  
*That's good. Where can I get information?*

**Kollege:**  
Bei der Personalabteilung.  
*At the HR department.*

**Anna:**  
Danke für den Tipp.  
*Thanks for the tip.*

**Situation:**

*Max registers for a workshop.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Max:**

*I'd like to register for a workshop.*

*The workshop about presentation techniques.*

*Good, I'll check availability.*

Wann findet er statt?  
*When does it take place?*

**Personalabteilung:**  
Nächsten Freitag von neun bis zwölf Uhr.  
*Next Friday from nine to twelve o'clock.*

**Max:**  
Das passt mir gut.  
*That works well for me.*

**Personalabteilung:**  
Perfekt, ich melde Sie an.  
*Perfect, I'll register you.*

**Max:**  
Vielen Dank.  
*Thank you very much.*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Modal Verbs (möchten, können, sollen)

**English:** Modal verbs express wishes, ability, or obligation. They are used with another verb in the infinitive form.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich möchte mich für einen Workshop anmelden** - I'd like to register for a workshop
- **Wir können verschiedene Workshops anbieten** - We can offer various workshops
- **Sie sollten die Materialien lesen** - You should read the materials
- **Ich soll den Workshop evaluieren** - I'm supposed to evaluate the workshop

### Perfekt (Past Tense)

**English:** The Perfekt tense describes completed actions in the past, formed with "haben" or "sein" + past participle.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich habe viel gelernt** - I've learned a lot
- **Ich habe Verbesserungen bemerkt** - I've noticed improvements
- **Der Workshop hat sich gelohnt** - The workshop was worth it

### Accusative Case with Direct Objects

**English:** The accusative case is used for direct objects in German sentences.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich suche nach einem Workshop** - I'm looking for a workshop (accusative)
- **Ich wende die Methoden an** - I apply the methods (accusative)
- **Ich lese die Materialien** - I read the materials (accusative)

### Subordinate Clauses with "dass" and "weil"

**English:** Subordinate clauses provide additional information and are introduced by conjunctions like "dass" (that) or "weil" (because).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich hoffe, dass es hilfreich ist** - I hope that it's helpful
- **Der Workshop war gut, weil die Übungen praktisch waren** - The workshop was good because the exercises were practical
