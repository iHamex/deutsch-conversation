# Orte und Plätze / Locations and Places

---

## 001: Im Supermarkt / At the Supermarket

**Situation:**  
Maria fragt Lisa, wo sie ist.  
*Maria asks Lisa where she is.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Maria:**  
Hallo Lisa! Wo bist du?  
*Hello Lisa! Where are you?*

**Lisa:**  
Ich bin im Supermarkt.  
*I'm at the supermarket.*

**Maria:**  
Welcher Supermarkt?  
*Which supermarket?*

**Lisa:**  
Der große Supermarkt in der Hauptstraße.  
*The big supermarket on Main Street.*

**Maria:**  
Ah, ich kenne den. Was machst du dort?  
*Ah, I know that one. What are you doing there?*

**Lisa:**  
Ich kaufe Lebensmittel ein.  
*I'm buying groceries.*

**Maria:**  
Brauchst du Hilfe?  
*Do you need help?*

**Lisa:**  
Nein, danke. Ich bin gleich fertig.  
*No, thanks. I'll be done soon.*

**Maria:**  
Okay, bis später!  
*Okay, see you later!*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Asking "Where Are You?" with "wo bist du"

**English:** To ask where someone is, use "wo bist du?" (informal) or "wo sind Sie?" (formal), meaning "where are you?" This is the most common way to ask about someone's location.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Wo bist du?** - Where are you? (informal)
- **Wo bist du gerade?** - Where are you right now?
- **Wo sind Sie?** - Where are you? (formal)
- **Wo bist du?** - Where are you?

### Prepositions for Locations: "im", "am", "zu Hause"

**English:** German uses different prepositions for locations: "im" (in/at) for buildings, "am" (at) for specific places, and "zu Hause" (at home) as a special expression. These help specify where someone is.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich bin im Supermarkt** - I'm at the supermarket
- **Ich bin im Park** - I'm at the park
- **Ich bin am Bahnhof** - I'm at the train station
- **Ich bin zu Hause** - I'm at home

### Asking "Which" with "welcher/welche/welches"

**English:** To ask "which" place someone means, use "welcher" (masculine), "welche" (feminine), or "welches" (neuter) followed by the noun. This helps clarify which specific location is meant.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Welcher Supermarkt?** - Which supermarket?
- **Welches Café?** - Which café?
- **Welche Bibliothek?** - Which library?
- **Welches Restaurant?** - Which restaurant?

### Describing Activities at Locations

**English:** When telling someone where you are, you often explain what you're doing there. Use present tense verbs to describe your activity at that location.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich kaufe Lebensmittel ein** - I'm buying groceries
- **Ich mache einen Spaziergang** - I'm taking a walk
- **Ich trinke einen Kaffee** - I'm drinking a coffee
- **Ich lerne für eine Prüfung** - I'm studying for an exam
