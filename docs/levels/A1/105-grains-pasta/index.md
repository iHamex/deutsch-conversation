# Getreide und Nudeln / Grains and Pasta

---

## 001: Reis kaufen / Buying Rice

**Situation:**  
Anna kauft Reis im Supermarkt.  
*Anna buys rice at the supermarket.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Anna:**  
Entschuldigung, wo ist der Reis?  
*Excuse me, where is the rice?*

**Verkäufer:**  
Der Reis ist in Regal fünf.  
*The rice is in aisle five.*

**Anna:**  
Danke!  
*Thanks!*

**Anna:**  
Welche Reissorten haben Sie?  
*Which rice varieties do you have?*

**Verkäufer:**  
Wir haben Langkornreis und Rundkornreis.  
*We have long grain rice and short grain rice.*

**Anna:**  
Was ist der Unterschied?  
*What is the difference?*

**Verkäufer:**  
Langkornreis ist für Beilagen, Rundkornreis für Risotto.  
*Long grain rice is for side dishes, short grain rice for risotto.*

**Anna:**  
Ich nehme Langkornreis.  
*I'll take long grain rice.*

**Verkäufer:**  
Sehr gut.  
*Very good.*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Asking "Where" with "wo"

**English:** To ask where grains and pasta are located in a store, use "wo" (where) followed by "ist" (is) for singular items.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Wo ist der Reis?** - Where is the rice?
- **Der Reis ist in Regal fünf** - The rice is in aisle five
- **Hier sind die Nudeln** - Here is the pasta
- **Couscous ist hier** - Couscous is here

### Asking About Varieties with "welche Sorten"

**English:** To ask about different varieties of grains or pasta, use "welche Sorten haben Sie" (which varieties do you have) or "welche Nudeln haben Sie" (which pasta do you have).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Welche Reissorten haben Sie?** - Which rice varieties do you have?
- **Welche Sorten haben Sie?** - Which varieties do you have?
- **Wir haben verschiedene Sorten** - We have different varieties
- **Wir haben Spaghetti, Penne und Fusilli** - We have spaghetti, penne and fusilli

### Asking "What" with "was ist"

**English:** To ask what something is, use "was ist" (what is) followed by the item. This is useful when learning about unfamiliar grains or pasta.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Was ist Quinoa?** - What is quinoa?
- **Was ist der Unterschied?** - What is the difference?
- **Was ist Polenta?** - What is polenta?
- **Das ist ein Getreide** - That's a grain

### Expressing Need with "ich brauche"

**English:** To express that you need specific grains or pasta, use "ich brauche" (I need) followed by the items. This is useful when shopping for multiple items.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich brauche verschiedene Nudelsorten** - I need different pasta varieties
- **Welche Sorten möchten Sie?** - Which varieties would you like?
- **Spaghetti, Penne und Lasagneplatten** - Spaghetti, penne and lasagna sheets
- **Ich nehme beide** - I'll take both
