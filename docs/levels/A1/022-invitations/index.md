# Einladungen / Invitations

---

## 001: Einladung zum Kaffee / Invitation for Coffee

**Situation:**  
Lisa lädt ihre Freundin Maria zu einem Kaffee ein.  
*Lisa invites her friend Maria for a coffee.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Lisa:**  
Hallo Maria! Wie geht es dir?  
*Hello Maria! How are you?*

**Maria:**  
Hallo Lisa! Mir geht es gut, danke.  
*Hello Lisa! I'm doing well, thanks.*

**Lisa:**  
Hast du heute Nachmittag Zeit?  
*Do you have time this afternoon?*

**Maria:**  
Ja, ich habe Zeit. Warum?  
*Yes, I have time. Why?*

**Lisa:**  
Möchtest du einen Kaffee trinken?  
*Would you like to drink a coffee?*

**Maria:**  
Ja, gerne! Das ist eine gute Idee.  
*Yes, gladly! That's a good idea.*

**Lisa:**  
Super! Wo treffen wir uns?  
*Great! Where do we meet?*

**Maria:**  
Im Café am Marktplatz?  
*At the café at the market square?*

**Lisa:**  
Ja, perfekt! Um wie viel Uhr?  
*Yes, perfect! At what time?*

**Maria:**  
Um drei Uhr?  
*At three o'clock?*

**Lisa:**  
Ja, um drei Uhr. Bis gleich!  
*Yes, at three o'clock. See you soon!*

**Maria:**  
Bis gleich!  
*See you soon!*

## 003: Einladung zum Abendessen / Dinner Invitation

**Situation:**  
Anna lädt ihre Nachbarin Petra zum Abendessen ein.  
*Anna invites her neighbor Petra for dinner.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Anna:**  
Guten Tag, Petra!  
*Good day, Petra!*

**Petra:**  
Guten Tag, Anna! Wie geht es Ihnen?  
*Good day, Anna! How are you?*

**Anna:**  
Sehr gut, danke. Haben Sie am Freitag Zeit?  
*Very good, thanks. Do you have time on Friday?*

**Petra:**  
Ja, ich habe Zeit. Was haben Sie vor?  
*Yes, I have time. What do you have in mind?*

**Anna:**  
Möchten Sie bei mir zu Abend essen?  
*Would you like to have dinner at my place?*

**Petra:**  
Oh, das ist sehr nett! Ja, gerne!  
*Oh, that's very nice! Yes, gladly!*

**Anna:**  
Schön! Kommen Sie um sechs Uhr?  
*Nice! Come at six o'clock?*

**Petra:**  
Ja, um sechs Uhr. Das passt gut.  
*Yes, at six o'clock. That works well.*

**Anna:**  
Perfekt! Ich koche etwas Leckeres.  
*Perfect! I'll cook something delicious.*

**Petra:**  
Das freut mich sehr. Vielen Dank für die Einladung!  
*I'm very happy about that. Thank you very much for the invitation!*

**Anna:**  
Gern geschehen! Bis Freitag!  
*You're welcome! See you Friday!*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Modal Verb "möchten" (would like)

**English:** "Möchten" is a modal verb meaning "would like" and is used to make polite invitations and requests. It's one of the most common ways to invite someone in German.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Möchtest du einen Kaffee trinken?** - Would you like to drink a coffee?
- **Möchten Sie bei mir zu Abend essen?** - Would you like to have dinner at my place?
- **Möchtest du am Sonntag ins Kino gehen?** - Would you like to go to the cinema on Sunday?
- **Möchtest du mitkommen?** - Would you like to come along?

### Asking About Time with "um" and "wann"

**English:** To ask about specific times, use "um" (at) with the time. To ask "when" something happens, use "wann" (when).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Um wie viel Uhr?** - At what time?
- **Um drei Uhr** - At three o'clock
- **Um sieben Uhr abends** - At seven o'clock in the evening
- **Wann kommst du?** - When are you coming?

### Prepositions for Meeting Places

**English:** German uses different prepositions to indicate where people meet. Common ones include "am" (at), "im" (in), "vor" (in front of), and "bei" (at someone's place).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Im Café am Marktplatz** - At the café at the market square
- **Bei mir zu Hause** - At my home
- **Vor dem Fitnessstudio** - In front of the gym
- **Am Parkeingang** - At the park entrance

### Expressing Acceptance with "gerne"

**English:** "Gerne" (gladly) is the most common way to accept an invitation politely in German. It can be used alone or with "ja" (yes).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ja, gerne!** - Yes, gladly!
- **Ja, sehr gerne!** - Yes, very gladly!
- **Das ist sehr nett! Ja, gerne!** - That's very nice! Yes, gladly!
