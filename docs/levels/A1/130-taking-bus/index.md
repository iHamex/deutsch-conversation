# Mit dem Bus fahren / Taking Bus

---

## 001: Busfahrkarte kaufen / Buying Bus Ticket

**Situation:**  
Lisa kauft eine Busfahrkarte beim Fahrer.  
*Lisa buys a bus ticket from the driver.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Lisa:**  
Guten Tag! Eine Fahrkarte, bitte.  
*Good day! One ticket, please.*

**Busfahrer:**  
Wohin möchten Sie fahren?  
*Where would you like to go?*

**Lisa:**  
Zum Bahnhof, bitte.  
*To the train station, please.*

**Busfahrer:**  
Das macht zwei Euro fünfzig.  
*That's two euros fifty.*

**Lisa:**  
Hier bitte.  
*Here please.*

**Busfahrer:**  
Vielen Dank!  
*Thank you very much!*

**Lisa:**  
Bitte schön!  
*You're welcome!*

## 003: Bus verpassen / Missing the Bus

**Situation:**

*Anna misses her bus.*

*Excuse me, when does the next bus come?*

**Angestellter:**

*In ten minutes.*

**Anna:**

*I missed my bus.*

**Angestellter:**  
Das tut mir leid.  
*I'm sorry to hear that.*

**Anna:**  
Ist das ein Problem?  
*Is that a problem?*

**Angestellter:**  
Nein, der nächste Bus kommt bald.  
*No, the next bus comes soon.*

**Anna:**  
Das ist gut.  
*That's good.*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Asking "Where" with "wohin möchten Sie fahren"

**English:** When taking the bus, you need to tell the driver where you want to go. Use "wohin möchten Sie fahren?" (where would you like to go?) or simply state your destination with "zum" (to the) or "zur" (to the).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Wohin möchten Sie fahren?** - Where would you like to go?
- **Zum Bahnhof, bitte** - To the train station, please
- **Zum Zentrum** - To the center
- **Zum Park** - To the park

### Asking "When" with "wann kommt"

**English:** To ask when the bus arrives, use "wann kommt der Bus?" (when does the bus come?) or "wann kommt der nächste Bus?" (when does the next bus come?). Answer with "in" (in) followed by the time.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Wann kommt der nächste Bus?** - When does the next bus come?
- **In zehn Minuten** - In ten minutes
- **In fünfzehn Minuten** - In fifteen minutes
- **Der nächste Bus kommt bald** - The next bus comes soon

### Asking About Bus Numbers with "welcher Bus"

**English:** To ask which bus goes to your destination, use "welcher Bus fährt" (which bus goes) followed by the destination. You can also ask "fährt dieser Bus" (does this bus go) to confirm you're on the right bus.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Welcher Bus fährt zum Zentrum?** - Which bus goes to the center?
- **Der Bus Nummer fünf** - Bus number five
- **Fährt dieser Bus zum Park?** - Does this bus go to the park?
- **Welche Haltestelle suchen Sie?** - Which stop are you looking for?

### Expressing Problems with "ich habe verpasst" and "der Bus ist"

**English:** When something goes wrong, use "ich habe verpasst" (I missed) or describe the situation with "der Bus ist" (the bus is) followed by the problem like "voll" (full) or "verspätet" (delayed).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich habe meinen Bus verpasst** - I missed my bus
- **Der Bus ist voll** - The bus is full
- **Der Bus ist verspätet** - The bus is delayed
- **Warum hält der Bus nicht?** - Why doesn't the bus stop?
