# In der Eisdiele / At Ice Cream Shop

---

## 001: Einfaches Eis / Simple Ice Cream

**Situation:**  
Anna bestellt ein Eis in der Eisdiele.  
*Anna orders an ice cream at the ice cream shop.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Verkäufer:**  
Guten Tag! Was möchten Sie?  
*Good day! What would you like?*

**Anna:**  
Guten Tag! Ich möchte ein Eis, bitte.  
*Good day! I would like an ice cream, please.*

**Verkäufer:**  
Gerne. Welche Sorte möchten Sie?  
*Gladly. Which flavor would you like?*

**Anna:**  
Was haben Sie?  
*What do you have?*

**Verkäufer:**  
Wir haben Schokolade, Vanille, Erdbeere und viele andere Sorten.  
*We have chocolate, vanilla, strawberry and many other flavors.*

**Anna:**  
Ich nehme Schokolade, bitte.  
*I'll take chocolate, please.*

**Verkäufer:**  
Sehr gut. Eine Kugel oder zwei?  
*Very good. One scoop or two?*

**Anna:**  
Eine Kugel, bitte.  
*One scoop, please.*

**Verkäufer:**  
Das macht zwei Euro.  
*That makes two euros.*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Numbers with Ice Cream Orders

**English:** When ordering ice cream, numbers are used to specify how many scoops you want. Numbers are used with "Kugel" (scoop).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Eine Kugel** - One scoop
- **Zwei Kugeln** - Two scoops
- **Drei Kugeln** - Three scoops
- **Vier Eis** - Four ice creams

### "in der Waffel" and "im Becher" (in cone/cup)

**English:** "In der Waffel" (in a cone) and "im Becher" (in a cup) are essential phrases when ordering ice cream. "Der" is dative case for feminine nouns, "im" is dative for masculine/neuter.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **In der Waffel oder im Becher?** - In a cone or in a cup?
- **In der Waffel, bitte** - In a cone, please
- **Im Becher, bitte** - In a cup, please

### Flavor Names as Nouns

**English:** Ice cream flavors in German are typically nouns and are capitalized. They can be used with or without "Eis" (ice cream).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Schokolade** - Chocolate
- **Vanille** - Vanilla
- **Erdbeere** - Strawberry
- **Mango-Eis** - Mango ice cream

### "mit" for Additions

**English:** "Mit" (with) is used to specify additions to ice cream, like cream or toppings.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Mit Sahne** - With cream
- **Mit zwei Kugeln** - With two scoops
- **Eis mit Sahne** - Ice cream with cream
