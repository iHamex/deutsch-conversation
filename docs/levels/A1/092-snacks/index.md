# Snacks / Snacks

---

## 001: Chips kaufen / Buying Chips

**Situation:**  
Anna kauft Chips im Supermarkt.  
*Anna buys chips at the supermarket.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Anna:**  
Ich möchte Chips, bitte.  
*I would like chips, please.*

**Verkäufer:**  
Gerne. Welche Chips möchten Sie?  
*Gladly. Which chips would you like?*

**Anna:**  
Was haben Sie?  
*What do you have?*

**Verkäufer:**  
Wir haben normale Chips, Paprika-Chips und Käse-Chips.  
*We have normal chips, paprika chips and cheese chips.*

**Anna:**  
Ich nehme Paprika-Chips, bitte.  
*I'll take paprika chips, please.*

**Verkäufer:**  
Sehr gut. Groß oder klein?  
*Very good. Large or small?*

**Anna:**  
Groß, bitte.  
*Large, please.*

**Verkäufer:**  
Das macht drei Euro.  
*That makes three euros.*

## 010: Snacks teilen / Sharing Snacks

**Situation:**  
Zwei Freunde teilen Snacks.  
*Two friends share snacks.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Tom:**  
Ich habe Chips. Möchtest du auch?  
*I have chips. Would you also like some?*

**Lisa:**  
Ja, gerne. Danke.  
*Yes, gladly. Thank you.*

**Tom:**  
Bitte. Hier sind die Chips.  
*You're welcome. Here are the chips.*

**Lisa:**  
Die Chips schmecken gut.  
*The chips taste good.*

**Tom:**  
Ja, sie sind lecker.  
*Yes, they're delicious.*

**Lisa:**  
Möchtest du auch etwas von meinen Keksen?  
*Would you also like some of my cookies?*

**Tom:**  
Ja, gerne. Danke.  
*Yes, gladly. Thank you.*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Snack Names as Nouns

**English:** All snack names in German are nouns and are capitalized. They often have specific articles (der/die/das).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **die Chips** - the chips
- **die Schokolade** - the chocolate
- **die Nüsse** - the nuts
- **die Kekse** - the cookies

### "als Snack" (as a snack)

**English:** "Als Snack" means "as a snack" and is used to specify the purpose of eating something.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich esse Obst als Snack** - I eat fruit as a snack
- **Als Snack esse ich Chips** - As a snack I eat chips

### Taste Expressions: "schmecken" and "lecker"

**English:** "Schmecken" (to taste) and "lecker" (delicious) are essential when talking about snacks.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Die Chips schmecken gut** - The chips taste good
- **Sie sind lecker** - They're delicious
- **Das schmeckt** - That tastes good

### Sharing Expressions with "auch"

**English:** "Auch" (also/too) is used when offering or sharing snacks with others.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Möchtest du auch?** - Would you also like some?
- **Ich möchte auch** - I would also like
- **Auch gerne** - Also gladly
