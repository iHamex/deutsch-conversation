# Reservierungen machen / Making Reservations

---

## 001: Telefonische Reservierung / Phone Reservation

**Situation:**  
Anna ruft ein Restaurant an, um einen Tisch zu reservieren.  
*Anna calls a restaurant to reserve a table.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Restaurant:**  
Guten Tag, Restaurant Sonne.  
*Good day, Restaurant Sonne.*

**Anna:**  
Guten Tag. Ich möchte einen Tisch reservieren.  
*Good day. I would like to reserve a table.*

**Restaurant:**  
Gerne. Für wann möchten Sie den Tisch?  
*Gladly. For when would you like the table?*

**Anna:**  
Für heute Abend, bitte.  
*For this evening, please.*

**Restaurant:**  
Für wie viele Personen?  
*For how many people?*

**Anna:**  
Für zwei Personen.  
*For two people.*

**Restaurant:**  
Gut. Um wie viel Uhr?  
*Good. At what time?*

**Anna:**  
Um sieben Uhr, bitte.  
*At seven o'clock, please.*

**Restaurant:**  
Perfekt. Wie ist Ihr Name?  
*Perfect. What is your name?*

**Anna:**  
Anna Müller.  
*Anna Müller.*

**Restaurant:**  
Sehr gut, Frau Müller. Der Tisch ist reserviert.  
*Very good, Mrs. Müller. The table is reserved.*

*Lisa has to cancel her reservation.*

*Good day. I would like to cancel a reservation.*

*Gladly. What is your name?*

Lisa Hoffmann.  
*Lisa Hoffmann.*

**Restaurant:**

*I see the reservation for this evening. Do you really want to cancel?*

*Yes, unfortunately. I'm sick.*

**Restaurant:**

*I'm sorry. Get well soon!*

*Thank you. Can we maybe make a new appointment?*

**Restaurant:**  
Natürlich. Wann passt es Ihnen?  
*Of course. When does it suit you?*

**Lisa:**  
Vielleicht nächste Woche?  
*Maybe next week?*

**Restaurant:**  
Gut, rufen Sie einfach an.  
*Good, just call.*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Time Expressions with "um" (at)

**English:** "Um" is used to express "at" a specific time in German. It's essential for making reservations and appointments.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Um sieben Uhr** - At seven o'clock
- **Um halb acht** - At half past seven
- **Um acht Uhr** - At eight o'clock
- **Um halb neun** - At half past eight

### Future Time with "für" (for)

**English:** "Für" is used to indicate "for" a specific time or date when making reservations. It's followed by time expressions.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Für heute Abend** - For this evening
- **Für morgen Abend** - For tomorrow evening
- **Für nächsten Samstag** - For next Saturday
- **Für zwei Personen** - For two people

### Modal Verb "möchten" for Requests

**English:** "Möchten" (would like) is the polite way to make requests in German, especially when making reservations.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich möchte einen Tisch reservieren** - I would like to reserve a table
- **Ich möchte meine Reservierung bestätigen** - I would like to confirm my reservation
- **Ich möchte einen Tisch stornieren** - I would like to cancel a table
- **Ich hätte einen Wunsch** - I would have a request

### Question Words for Reservations

**English:** Common question words used when making reservations include "wann" (when), "wie viele" (how many), and "wie" (how/what).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Für wann möchten Sie den Tisch?** - For when would you like the table?
- **Für wie viele Personen?** - For how many people?
- **Um welche Uhrzeit?** - At what time?
- **Wie ist Ihr Name?** - What is your name?
