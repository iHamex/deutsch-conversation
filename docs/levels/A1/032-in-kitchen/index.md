# In der Küche / In the Kitchen

---

## 001: Kochen / Cooking

**Situation:**  
Maria kocht mit Lisa zusammen.  
*Maria cooks together with Lisa.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Lisa:**  
Was kochst du?  
*What are you cooking?*

**Maria:**  
Ich koche Nudeln.  
*I'm cooking pasta.*

**Lisa:**  
Das klingt gut. Brauchst du Hilfe?  
*That sounds good. Do you need help?*

**Maria:**  
Ja, kannst du die Tomaten schneiden?  
*Yes, can you cut the tomatoes?*

**Lisa:**  
Ja, gerne! Wo ist das Messer?  
*Yes, gladly! Where is the knife?*

**Maria:**  
Im Schrank, links.  
*In the cabinet, left.*

**Lisa:**  
Ah, ich sehe es.  
*Ah, I see it.*

**Maria:**  
Danke für deine Hilfe!  
*Thanks for your help!*

**Lisa:**  
Gern geschehen!  
*You're welcome!*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Kitchen Activities with Present Tense Verbs

**English:** Common kitchen activities use present tense verbs: "kochen" (to cook), "backen" (to bake), "spülen" (to wash), "aufräumen" (to clean up). These verbs describe what you do in the kitchen.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich koche Nudeln** - I'm cooking pasta
- **Ich mache Frühstück** - I'm making breakfast
- **Ich spüle das Geschirr** - I'm washing the dishes
- **Ich backe einen Kuchen** - I'm baking a cake

### Asking for Help in the Kitchen with "kannst du"

**English:** When cooking together, use "kannst du" (can you) to ask for help with specific tasks. This is polite and direct for kitchen activities.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Kannst du die Tomaten schneiden?** - Can you cut the tomatoes?
- **Kannst du den Kaffee machen?** - Can you make the coffee?
- **Kannst du die Teller holen?** - Can you get the plates?
- **Kannst du den Tisch abwischen?** - Can you wipe the table?

### Kitchen Items with Prepositions

**English:** When asking where kitchen items are, use prepositions like "im" (in), "auf" (on), "unter" (under), "über" (above) followed by the location. This helps find things in the kitchen.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Wo ist das Messer?** - Where is the knife?
- **Im Schrank, links** - In the cabinet, left
- **Auf dem Herd** - On the stove
- **Unter der Spüle** - Under the sink

### Expressing What You're Cooking with "es gibt"

**English:** To say what's for a meal, use "es gibt" (there is) followed by the food items. This is a common way to describe meals in German.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Was gibt es zum Frühstück?** - What's for breakfast?
- **Brot, Eier und Kaffee** - Bread, eggs, and coffee
- **Was gibt es?** - What's for dinner?
- **Fisch mit Kartoffeln** - Fish with potatoes
