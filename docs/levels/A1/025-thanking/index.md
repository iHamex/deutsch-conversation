# Danken / Thanking

---

## 001: Für Hilfe danken / Thanking for Help

**Situation:**  
Maria bedankt sich bei Lisa für ihre Hilfe.  
*Maria thanks Lisa for her help.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Lisa:**  
Hier ist dein Buch.  
*Here is your book.*

**Maria:**  
Vielen Dank! Das ist sehr nett von dir.  
*Thank you very much! That's very nice of you.*

**Lisa:**  
Gern geschehen!  
*You're welcome!*

**Maria:**  
Du hast mir wirklich geholfen.  
*You really helped me.*

**Lisa:**  
Kein Problem. Freut mich.  
*No problem. Glad to help.*

**Maria:**  
Danke nochmal!  
*Thanks again!*

**Lisa:**  
Bitte sehr!  
*You're welcome!*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Different Ways to Say "Thank You"

**English:** German has several ways to express thanks, from casual to formal. "Danke" is casual, "danke schön" is more polite, and "vielen Dank" is formal and emphatic.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Danke** - Thanks
- **Danke schön** - Thank you very much
- **Vielen Dank** - Thank you very much
- **Danke nochmal** - Thanks again

### Responding to Thanks with "Gern geschehen" and "Bitte sehr"

**English:** The most common responses to "danke" are "gern geschehen" (you're welcome), "bitte sehr" (you're welcome), and "kein Problem" (no problem). These show that you're happy to help.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Gern geschehen!** - You're welcome!
- **Bitte sehr!** - You're welcome!
- **Kein Problem** - No problem
- **Freut mich** - Glad to help

### Expressing Gratitude with "nett von dir" and "hilfreich"

**English:** To express that someone's action was kind or helpful, use phrases like "nett von dir" (nice of you) or "hilfreich" (helpful). This shows appreciation for the person's character.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Das ist sehr nett von dir** - That's very nice of you
- **Du bist sehr hilfsbereit** - You're very helpful
- **Du hast mir wirklich geholfen** - You really helped me
- **Das ist nett von dir zu sagen** - That's nice of you to say

### Thanking for Specific Things

**English:** When thanking for something specific, use "für" (for) followed by the thing you're thanking for. This makes your gratitude more specific and meaningful.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Vielen Dank für die Einladung** - Thank you very much for the invitation
- **Danke für deine Hilfe** - Thanks for your help
- **Vielen Dank für das Geschenk** - Thank you very much for the gift
- **Danke für deine Geduld** - Thanks for your patience
