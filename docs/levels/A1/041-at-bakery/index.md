# In der Bäckerei / At the Bakery

---

## 001: Brot kaufen / Buying Bread

**Situation:**  
Maria kauft Brot in der Bäckerei.  
*Maria buys bread at the bakery.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Bäcker:**  
Guten Tag! Kann ich Ihnen helfen?  
*Good day! Can I help you?*

**Maria:**  
Guten Tag! Ich möchte ein Brot, bitte.  
*Good day! I would like a bread, please.*

**Bäcker:**  
Welches Brot möchten Sie?  
*Which bread would you like?*

**Maria:**  
Ein Vollkornbrot, bitte.  
*A whole grain bread, please.*

**Bäcker:**  
Sehr gut. Sonst noch etwas?  
*Very good. Anything else?*

**Maria:**  
Nein, danke. Das ist alles.  
*No, thanks. That's everything.*

**Bäcker:**  
Das macht drei Euro fünfzig.  
*That's three euros fifty.*

**Maria:**  
Hier bitte.  
*Here please.*

**Bäcker:**  
Vielen Dank!  
*Thank you very much!*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Ordering at the Bakery with "ich möchte"

**English:** To order at a bakery, use "ich möchte" (I would like) followed by the item you want. This is polite and commonly used in shops and bakeries.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich möchte ein Brot, bitte** - I would like a bread, please
- **Ich möchte Brötchen, bitte** - I would like rolls, please
- **Ich möchte einen Kuchen** - I would like a cake
- **Ich möchte eine Torte bestellen** - I would like to order a cake

### Asking About Prices with "wie viel kostet"

**English:** To ask how much something costs, use "wie viel kostet" (how much does ... cost) followed by the item. The answer uses "kostet" (costs) followed by the price.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Wie viel kostet ein Brot?** - How much does a bread cost?
- **Ein Brot kostet drei Euro fünfzig** - A bread costs three euros fifty
- **Wie viel kosten die Brötchen?** - How much do the rolls cost?
- **Das macht zusammen fünf Euro** - That's five euros together

### Asking "Which" with "welche/welcher/welches"

**English:** When choosing items at the bakery, use "welche" (which - feminine), "welcher" (which - masculine), or "welches" (which - neuter) followed by the noun. This helps specify what you want.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Welches Brot möchten Sie?** - Which bread would you like?
- **Welche Sorte?** - Which kind?
- **Welchen Kuchen möchten Sie?** - Which cake would you like?
- **Welche Brotsorten haben Sie?** - Which bread varieties do you have?

### Expressing Freshness and Quality

**English:** When buying bread, you often ask about freshness or quality. Use "ist es frisch?" (is it fresh?), "wann wurde es gebacken?" (when was it baked?), or "was können Sie empfehlen?" (what can you recommend?).

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ist das Brot frisch?** - Is the bread fresh?
- **Es ist ganz frisch** - It's very fresh
- **Wann wurde es gebacken?** - When was it baked?
- **Was können Sie empfehlen?** - What can you recommend?
