# In der Apotheke / At Pharmacy

---

## 001: Kopfschmerztabletten / Headache Tablets

**Situation:**  
Maria kauft Tabletten gegen Kopfschmerzen.  
*Maria buys tablets for headaches.*

### Dialog / Dialogue

**Apotheker:**  
Guten Tag! Kann ich Ihnen helfen?  
*Good day! Can I help you?*

**Maria:**  
Guten Tag! Ich habe Kopfschmerzen.  
*Good day! I have a headache.*

**Apotheker:**  
Wir haben Tabletten dagegen.  
*We have tablets for that.*

**Maria:**  
Das ist gut.  
*That's good.*

**Apotheker:**  
Wie viele Tabletten möchten Sie?  
*How many tablets would you like?*

**Maria:**  
Eine Packung, bitte.  
*One package, please.*

**Apotheker:**  
Sehr gut. Das macht fünf Euro.  
*Very good. That's five euros.*

**Maria:**  
Hier bitte.  
*Here please.*

**Apotheker:**  
Gute Besserung!  
*Get well soon!*

## Grammatik / Grammar

### Expressing Health Problems with "ich habe"

**English:** To say you have a health problem, use "ich habe" (I have) followed by the condition. This is the most common way to describe symptoms at a pharmacy.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Ich habe Kopfschmerzen** - I have a headache
- **Ich habe Husten** - I have a cough
- **Ich habe eine Erkältung** - I have a cold
- **Ich habe Rückenschmerzen** - I have back pain

### Asking for Help with "was hilft" and "haben Sie"

**English:** When you need something for a health problem, ask "was hilft dagegen?" (what helps against it?) or "haben Sie etwas dagegen?" (do you have something for it?). You can also ask "haben Sie" (do you have) followed by the item you need.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Was hilft dagegen?** - What helps against it?
- **Haben Sie Pflaster?** - Do you have bandages?
- **Haben Sie Vitamine?** - Do you have vitamins?
- **Haben Sie etwas dagegen?** - Do you have something for it?

### Asking for Recommendations with "was empfehlen Sie" and "welche empfehlen Sie"

**English:** When you need advice at the pharmacy, use "was empfehlen Sie?" (what do you recommend?) or "welche empfehlen Sie?" (which do you recommend?) followed by the type of product.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Welche empfehlen Sie?** - Which do you recommend?
- **Was ist gut?** - What is good?
- **Welche ist gut?** - Which is good?
- **Können Sie mich beraten?** - Can you advise me?

### Expressing Sympathy with "das tut mir leid"

**English:** When someone tells you about a health problem, respond with "das tut mir leid" (I'm sorry to hear that) to show sympathy. This is polite and common in German pharmacies.

**Examples from conversations:**

- **Das tut mir leid** - I'm sorry to hear that
- **Gute Besserung!** - Get well soon!
